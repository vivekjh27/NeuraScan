Understanding Brain Tumors

What is a Brain Tumor?

A brain tumor is an abnormal mass of cells in your brain. Your skull is very rigid, so any growth inside this restricted space can cause problems. Brain tumors can be cancerous (malignant) or non-cancerous (benign). When benign or malignant tumors grow, they can cause the pressure inside your skull to increase, which can cause brain damage and be life-threatening.

Primary brain tumors originate in your brain. Secondary (metastatic) brain tumors result from cancer that starts elsewhere in your body and spreads to your brain.

⚗️

How They Form

Brain tumors form when normal cells undergo genetic mutations causing uncontrolled division. This may be triggered by radiation, inherited conditions, or occur spontaneously.

🚨

Why Early Detection Matters

Early-stage brain tumors respond significantly better to treatment. Detection before neurological damage occurs preserves quality of life and improves survival rates.

🔍

Common Symptoms

Severe headaches, blurred vision, loss of sensation, seizures, difficulty with balance, speech problems, confusion, or personality changes can all signal a brain tumor.

AI in Diagnosis

Deep learning models trained on thousands of MRI scans detect subtle patterns imperceptible to the human eye, serving as a powerful second opinion alongside clinical evaluation.
